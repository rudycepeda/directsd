Limitations

Polynomial design methods for sampled-data systems developed in [1] and [2] have some limitations:
  1. Controller K is a SISO system.
  2. Transfer matrices P11(s), P21(s), and P22(s) must be strictly proper, P12(s) must be at least proper.
The most restrictive is the first assumption. The reason is that polynomial design techniques are most effective for scalar problems. As for designing multivariable controllers, it is much miore reliable to use the state-space methods [3].

The second limitation ensures that the problems under consideration are well-posed. This means that the inputs are affacted only by finite signals and the outputs are finite also (no impulsive modes).

References

[1] Rosenwasser, E.N., and B.P. Lampe, Computer Controlled Systems: Analysis and Design with Process-orientated Models. London: Springer-Verlag, 2000.

[2] Polyakov, K.Yu., Polynomial Design of Optimal Sampled-data Control Systems, Dr. Sc. Thesis, State University of Ocean Technology, St. Petersburg, 2006 [in Russian].

[3] Chen, T., and B.A. Francis, Optimal Sampled-Data Control Systems. Berlin Heidelberg New York: Springer-Verlag, 1995.
